## Gault&Millau's review

At the foot of the Petrovaradin fortress, housed within the storied architecture of an old railway station, lies a sanctuary of French sophistication. This is more than a restaurant; it is an inspiring transit point between the landscapes of European haute cuisine and the seasonal soul of Vojvodina. The journey begins with the house-made bread, carved tableside, followed by a brilliant overture of flavors: intense baked brie with honey, pheasant pâté with currant jelly, and expertly prepared bone marrow. The menu continues to intrigue with delicate veal and goose liver with green apple, terrine of boiled drumstick with spinach hummus and perch pâté, foie gras paired on celery puree, and a vibrant beetroot and Roquefort risotto. The pleasure is rounded off by a selected selection of European wine labels with suggestions for pairing with dishes, as well as an excellent selection of alcoholic beverages, with a special focus on rakija. Velvety veal blanquette and coq au vin bring all the luxury of classic French cuisine, and the local culinary skills are confirmed by the soft venison steaks with beurre blanc sauce. Perfect desserts follow the refined sensibility, from the hazelnut parfait to the original version of Crepes Suzette.

#### [NACIONALNI RESTORAN BELA REKA](/en/restaurants/nacionalni-restoran-bela-reka)

#### [GIULIA](/en/restaurants/giulia-%E2%80%93-italijanski-restoran) 

Giulia is located in Galerija mall, with a fabulous view of the river, and a rich offer of authentic flavors of Italian cuisine. The atmosphere is lively and pleasant; together with the good food it helps one forget the busy rhythm of the mall. The choice of dishes is really challenging, but a few plates especially attract attention. For starters, try the sea bass ceviche, a hearty combination of a hefty medallion of raw fish, avocado, citrus sauce and fresh greens that brings a slight bitter note. The combination is refreshing and reminiscent of what made this Latin American classic a world favorite. The next dish worth trying is puttanesca with octopus. The rich red sauce, capers and olives give a distinctive salty-bitter aroma, while the tender octopus gives this traditional recipe a new dimension and ignites the imagination to unimagined limits. Homemade pasta is cooked perfectly al dente and masterly binds all the flavors on the plate. The pistachio cake brings a little spectacle because, when you break the white chocolate crust, you can dig into berries and a divine cream that is difficult to describe. This gem and opulent finish will bring you back to this place.
