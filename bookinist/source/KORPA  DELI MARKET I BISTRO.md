## Gault&Millau's review

Overlooking a wide, vibrant boulevard in Novi Sad, this gem welcomes guests with an electric atmosphere, friendly staff and a menu that defies easy categorization We began our exploration with a duo of inventive appetizers: oyster mushrooms with leeks and feta cheese and rolled carpaccio. Delicate oyster mushrooms with earthy notes, slightly sweet, with roasted leeks that added flavor and feta, which perfectly countered the sweetness of the leeks and the full nuances of the mushrooms. The carpaccio was extremely fresh, chilled, rolled so that it was streaked with the delicious fat of the meat. Cut into nice round slices, it was topped with a sauce made from roasted bone marrow, dried apricots, Parmesan and enhanced with apple slices that added sweetness and citrus notes. The pièce de résistance, however, was the parmesan-crusted leg of lamb. The meat is rolled in panko and Parmesan and fried until golden and crispy. It was placed on a pillow of mashed potatoes, topped with two shavings of Parmesan cheese, and finished with a surprisingly harmonious sweet oyster sauce. The dish is a brilliant interplay of crunch, salt, and sea - a combination we cannot recommend highly enough.

Chef

#### [RESTORAN CVETA](/en/restaurants/restoran-cveta) 

Housed within the storied walls of the former Cveta Dabić factory on the very banks of the river, this spacious restaurant is a testament to Užice’s creative rebirth. The menu artfully balances international flair with national soul. Begin with the fried brie, elegantly served with a tart blueberry reduction on a bed of garden greens, or the grilled oyster mushrooms nestled on crisp, artisanal dough. The menu continues with a diverse selection of risottos, most notably with salmon and dill and the black with squid and prawns. We opted for the pastas, unusual, spicy Hot Cheddar tagliatelle with hot peppers, basil, cherry tomatoes and crispy prosciutto. Among the meat specialties, we chose an excellent grilled pork fillet served with hollandaise sauce on a mix of mushrooms. The local offer is also represented by smoked lamb shank sous vide, veal under sač and pljeskavica with kajmak. The rest of the menu includes steaks and a serious selection of fish - tuna and salmon, sea bass and squid, octopus and shrimp, and trout and perch fillets. Finally, we suggest a big cream puff with red fruit topping, pistachio mousse with raspberries and biscuit, or chocolate brownies in white chocolate and orange sauce.

#### [SOKAČE](/en/restaurants/restoran-soka%C4%8Ce) 

Sokače is a restaurant with tradition and hospitality that encompasses service, experience of the table and a full, homely taste. The warm interior is filled with details from past times and photographs of famous guests. The menu remains faithful to traditional cuisine and authentic, almost forgotten flavors, with a wide range of dishes. A showcase with fresh meat prepared to order is an introduction to a serious barbecue repertoire. The menu is also based on homemade pies, gibanica and cornbread, kajmak, cheeses, cold appetizers and spreads, soups, dishes under sač and cooked classics with regional accents from the south and east of Serbia. The character of the house is shown well in order - pepper cream soup, roasted pepper on kajmak, baked beans, vine leaf rolls with sour milk, ajmokac, lamb kapama, wedding cabbage and smoked pork shank. Desserts include fresh baklava with thin crusts, homemade dumplings with several types of jam, tufahija, floating island or vanilla slice. In the evening, they have live tamburica music. Your departure is marked by a final gesture of warmth: a house-made vanilica cookies and raspberry liqueur.

#### [ETNO BRVNARA GROŠ](/en/restaurants/etno-restoran-gros) 

Etno Brvnara Groš builds its identity around the conscious preservation and celebration of its own heritage. The ambiance is strongly marked by traditional elements - wood and stone throughout, and a large barrel within which tables are set, creating an impression that is both authentic and memorable. Staff, tableware, and the manner of service consistently follow the spirit of an older, unhurried time. Begin with rakija, then surrender to an abundant spread of fries, Moravian salad, warm buns, banica, creamy kajmak, and excellent veal soup. Exercise restraint, however - the list of local specialties is only just opening. Leskovac mućkalica served in a bun is essential, as is the onion patty, a barbecue classic with a perfect ratio of meat to fat. Uštipci, ćevapi, homemade sausages, and a generous bagel with kajmak complete the traditional roll call. The house specialties deserve particular attention: grilled peasant meat, tender pork leg with porcini mushrooms, and shepherd‘s steak with fried mushrooms and kajmak each carry the unmistakable stamp of the hearth. Homemade baklava arrives as the only logical conclusion to a meal of such honest and generous proportions.

#### [RESTORAN SIESTA](/en/restaurants/restoran-siesta) 

Positioned where the road from Belgrade meets the banks of the Đetinja River, this hotel is far more than a convenient rest stop; it is a refined gastronomic gateway to the Zlatibor region. Within its serene garden, the kitchen serves a sophisticated blend of international and local flavors. Special praise goes to the very friendly staff who know how to make recommendations, and when it comes to recommendations, be sure to try the local excellent peppers and cheese first. Broccoli potage or veal ragout soup will warm you up nicely, while the Đetinja plate represents all the specialties of the Užice region. An unusual breakthrough comes from the effective turkey in orange sauce, served with caramelized apples and zucchini. Then turkey with mlinci and shredded cured pork shank, unusual Madrelano ribs and steak in pepper sauce, and crispy chicken with seeds and medallions in kajmak. If you are into barbecue, the Siesta casserole for two is the right solution. Njeguš steak or sausage mix are also good choices. Our special recommendation for dessert is the Tres Leches cake, and the offer includes pancakes, chocolate souffle and baklava.

#### [VITINA IŽA](/en/restaurants/vitina-iza) 

Perched on the scenic route to Zavojno Lake, Vitina iža - or ‘Vita’s House’- is an impressive stone-built sanctuary offering panoramic views of Pirot. The spacious covered summer garden is ideal for any time of the day and evening in the summer season, while the interior is rustically decorated. The wine list, oriented towards Serbian wines, harmoniously follows the local menu, and there is also a good selection of rakija to whet the appetite, of course with flattened sausage and strained yoghurt and the essential trljanica with sheep’s cheese. No meal here is complete without their ‘magical’ bećarac and warm, oil-drenched buns dusted with crushed pepper. In the winter period, pork aspic, ajvar and sauerkraut, in the summer Moravian salad, and the obligatory đubek as a warm appetizer. Furthermore, you won’t go wrong choosing anything from the main part of the menu - from the grill, pljeskavica and pork tenderloin, to uštipak, ćevapi and sausages, and be sure to ask what is simmering under the sač. They are known for their great veal tails and soft beef tenderloin in kajmak. The option to order half-portions allows for a true tasting experience, perfectly capped off by Vita’s Cream - a decadent blend of two chocolates and sour cherry.

Beef cheeks in red wine sauce, and mućkalica, Vienna schnitzel and Karađorđe pork or turkey steak in the version called the Grande challenge are worthy of praise. As for fish, they have trout from Gostilje waterfalls, salmon and squid stuffed with Njeguš prosciutto and smoked cheese. Delicious beef ćevapi and excellent skewers, stuffed tenderloin in crépine and veal sweetbread arrive from the frill, and our advice is that you must accompany them with a homemade bun with kajmak. A special part of the menu is dedicated to aged meats, with sauces and side dishes. In the dessert section, there are juicy baklavas and chocolate trio cream, a delicate prune mousse, Tres Leches, or the indulgent Alexander pancakes in sabayon, with fruit and nuts.

This restaurant of impressive size is very popular, so reservations are recommended. For a true taste of the region, the goat cheese rolled in bacon with plums offers a masterful sweet-and-savory start. Followed by veal cream soup and beef soup, then cooked dishes, and then we move on to a serious selection of grilled dishes. The latter is crowned with mixed meat and Jevrem’s saber. Mali Balkan specialties range from creations with chicken - chicken strips in ajmokac sauce, chicken fantasia and amphora, to Roman cutlets in bacon, leek and parmesan sauce and breaded Čivija cutlet. The veal under the sač and the aromatic fillet in a wine sauce with mushrooms are excellent. There are also delicious sausages with cheese, and then the menu is divided into sections by type of meat. Tagliata steak Magnifico, boneless veal ribs, beef tenderloin in horseradish, turkey medallions in oyster mushroom sauce, Roquefort chicken or salmon with black sesame in dill sauce are just some of the dishes that attract attention. The dessert offer is as diverse.

The menu combines international cuisine with domestic classics. Two appealing potages open the meal - broccoli and gorgonzola, and carrot and orange - alongside a hearty homemade chicken soup. Salmon tartare, seasoned to your liking, or grilled goat cheese with salty and sweet sauces are a refined second step. The meat segment offers turkey in pesto-pistachio sauce, chicken with nuts, pork fillet with forest fruit dressing and pea purée, alongside classics such as Wiener schnitzel and Karađorđe steak. Ratatouille fillet, Tournedo Rossini, and ćevapi round out the savory offer, while local trout arrives with almonds and kajmak. The dessert display case is the meal‘s final flourish - vanilla slice, šampita, tiramisu, cheesecake, Moscow cake, Sachertorte, and the original Park cake among them. The desserts alone are reason enough to return.

This spot successfully pairs traditional Italian cuisine with innovative twists. Reservation is mandatory, and the wine list includes a selection of Italian and Serbian wineries. As an introduction, we recommend a very nice beef carpaccio, with the option of sea bass, and as a hot appetizer, mozzarella rolled with prosciutto or some of the bruschetta. #### [RESTORAN VUK](/en/restaurants/restoran-vuk) 

Vuk is one of the few remaining bastions where the nostalgic spirit of an authentic old tavern is still cherished. Located in the heart of the pedestrian zone near Knez Mihailova, it features old-fashioned waiters ready to charm every guest. Start with a cold rakija in a frosted glass alongside warm cheese pie, or crispy fried celery chips. Always check the daily dishes, which are prepared with gusto - from string beans with meat and sarma, to veal tongue in tomato sauce. National classics like creamy baked beans - with or without a sausage and outstanding Wiener Schnitzel, and Karađorđe steak are staples here. They also prepare a wide variety of grilled meats but their roasts truly stand out. The perfectly seasoned veal is roasted to an almost creamy texture that melts with every bite, and the pork shank is equally tender. To round off the meal, pancakes, baklava, or vanilla slice pair perfectly with their excellent brewed coffee. The consistent quality and traditional atmosphere make Vuk an unmistakable choice for those seeking a genuine taste of old Belgrade.
